Described as "a screenshot of the internet at this moment in time", Reddit opened a giant canvas in 2017 open to anyone in the world to edit one pixel at a time. However, people could only place one pixel every 5 minutes which meant collaboration was required to create anything meaningful. A huge amount of communities came together for the sole purpose of putting artwork together on the canvas. The picture on the right is the result of 3 days of planning, creation, and defense.

If you are interested in learning more about it (I find it pretty interesting), check out this article.
This semester of school, IÂ was the Philanthropy chair of my fraternity, Beta Sigma Psi. Most events on campus were very rinse and repeat:Â food, sports tournaments, and alumni fundraisers, but I wanted to do something different that brought a bit of fun to people as they were going about their day.
With the help of some friends, I created a 8x8 ft. canvas out of plywood and poster board. With a 1x1 inch grid on the poster board, people could fill with the stickers from the table on the right. We set it up and took it down each day for four days and watched it eventually get filled with art from students around campus.

This was also a philanthropy event that raised about $450 through sticker purchases from everyone that participated.
After the four days were over, my face was a little sore because I literally smiled so much during it. I definitely lost some of my voice also. I got to meet so many people and had a lot of fun interactions with people I've never seen and won't see again.

The best part of the whole week was hearing people say things like "this is one of the coolest thing I've seen someone do here" or "I really needed something like this". Even a lot of the people I didn't talk to smiled as they walked past since they recognized some of the 'cultural' bits people started on the board.

I'm not saying any of this because it stroked my ego or anything, but because it meant a lot that I was making a difference on campus and adding a bit of positivity throughout students' lives in a way they weren't expecting. (This was right before finals week haha.) I got to spread genuine joy and this event is something I'll remember for a while.
This is my steam profile as of October 28, 2023. Here, anyone else on Steam can comment on my profile, including pro Rocket League players... and phil who gave me a big hug.

A profile sign is like a digital autograph, so Rocket League personalities will sign players' profiles.
When I went to my first LAN event in the summer of 2022, I was super excited to meet all the people in the Rocket League scene - players, analysts, content creators, and others I had interacted with on platforms like Reddit, Twitter, and Twitch.
I wanted to give myself something to remember the event by, but being myself, I wanted to do something that people hadn't seen before. I knew all the players and personalities would be constantly surrounded by fans asking for them to sign stuff, so I didn't want to contribute to the sameness... but I still wanted what everyone else did lol.
I opened my steam profile, used inspect element to delete the comments, and printed out the page on a poster at Walmart leaving space for lots of people to sign their names.

Over the course of the event, IÂ tried to get as many people to give me a 'steam sign' and it made the event so much more enjoyable. IÂ still liked watching the games, but running around trying to meet people in between was one of the highlights of the week.
The poster was overwhelmingly successful and nearly everyone that signed it said they haven't seen that before and that it was one of the coolest things they've seen at an event. Every time I gave it to someone to sign, they almost immediately smiled and started laughing which was one of the goals of the poster in the first place - to give the personalities something interesting to break up the same old t-shirt signing or picture with a fan. (Not that there's anything wrong with that.)


A year and a half later, this poster is still hanging on my wall as I'm working on this and I imagine it will be with me for a while longer.
