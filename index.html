In and out of UX, I'm always trying to find a way to make life more interesting for others and myself because life's too short to do anything else. This translates into the following principles that guide my design work.
My Philosophy
1. Design is Fun
First and foremost, design shouldn't just have to be about solving a problem and making a process more efficient - it should also be about giving people a moment of joy or satisfaction. The products we create shouldn't just meet expectations, they should exceed them - and emotion is the best way to do that.
2. Design Challenges the Norm
It can be easy to point out one successful way that a problem has been solved in the past and replicate it, but designers have amazing opportunities when this exact situation comes up because it gives the chance to make something no one has seen before. We shouldn’t be afraid to do something completely differently just for fear of failure.
3. Design is Unnoticed
Creating an intuitive experience that just fits into someone's flow state is one of my favorite challenges within design. People have more important things to worry about than whether a door is push or pull. This could be seen as the opposite of my first principle, but making the task unnoticed means the potential for extra bits of excitement within it are even higher.
