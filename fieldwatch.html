Fostering Communication Between Crop Growers/Beekeepers and Pesticide Applicators

Purdue UX Experience Studio - FieldWatch

August 27, 2021 - December 13, 2021 (15 Weeks)

Team Size: 7 people

Fields can die because of miscommunication between pesticide applicators and crop growers.

Pesticides are designed to get sprayed on fields and kill the weeds while keeping the crops ok. Although it might work fine for one field, wind can take it over to adjacent fields killing its crops. The process of wind carrying over the pesticides is known as spray drift and it can also affect bees/apiaries nearby.

Freepik.com

Fieldwatch is a company that works to prevent spray drift by connecting pesticide applicators and crop growers/beekeepers.

in.driftwatch.org/map

With their online registry mapping tool, users can plot their properties on a map that is open for anyone to see. Each property has information that pesticide applicators can use to make sure spray drift won't harm nearby fields.

This probably sounds like a good system...

and it is! At least theoretically. Although the online mapping tool is very powerful, it's hard driving everyone to it through their main landing page because the concept of the company isn't very clear.

fieldwatch.com

Since all of the content for the original website wasn't put developed at the same time, parts were just thrown into the website as they got added. There isn't any flow between sections in the original design because of it, so it was a big focus for the redesign.

Final Design (Home Page)

Design Rationale

Due to all the different images/graphics, emphasis was taken away from the core mission of the company at the top of the landing page. By simplifying it and giving more attention to the mission, we were better able to lay a groundwork for the rest of the website.

There isn't really anywhere on the original website that explains how the registry system works, it's only referenced in the end goal. By showing how users interact with the registry mapping tool with our own animations, users can better understand their own end benefit from using it.

FieldWatch is only available in certain regions so we don't want to frustrate the user by telling them they can't sign up after they're already trying. If a user has FieldWatch in their area, they're able to keep scrolling to the final call-to-action, but if they don't then they're brought to a page that educates them on how to advocate for making their area registered.

Unlike the original page, we placed the registry portals at the bottom so users would understand what they're clicking on before they get sent off. We also made it more clear which registry is for which user. For example FieldCheck is for Applicators which isn't as obvious as BeeCheck. By separating the titles out with the heading and button inside the image, it's more apparent where users should click depending on their role.
